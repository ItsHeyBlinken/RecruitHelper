# College Recruiting Email Template Refinements (2024-2026)

Chris, your current templates are excellent—they follow the "Golden Rules" of recruiting: they are short, first-person, and avoid attachments. Based on current trends for the 2024-2026 cycles, here are a few tactical refinements to help you stand out in a coach's crowded inbox.

---

## 1. Subject Line "Power-Ups"
Coaches often check emails on their phones between practices. A "skimmable" subject line is your best friend.

*   **Current:** `[Grad Year] [Position] — [City, State] — [Your Name]`
*   **Refinement:** Include a **standout metric** or **physical attribute** immediately.
    *   *Example:* `2026 QB | 6'2" 190lbs | 3.9 GPA | [Your Name] | Highlights Attached`
    *   *Why:* Physical specs (height/weight) or a high GPA are often the first "filters" a coach uses.

---

## 2. Template-Specific Tweaks

### First Intro Email
*   **The Hook:** Move the "personalization" line higher. Instead of putting it in the middle, try making it the second sentence. 
*   **Refinement:** "I've been following [School Name]'s progress this season, especially the win against [Rival Name], and I love the energy of your [Position Group]."
*   **The Link:** Ensure your video link is on its own line for easy tapping on mobile.

### Follow-Up Email
*   **The "New Info" Rule:** Coaches appreciate persistence, but they love *progress*.
*   **Refinement:** Instead of just saying "following up," add one small piece of new information.
    *   *Example:* "I wanted to follow up and also share that I recently [increased my max lift / earned a 4.0 this semester / was named Captain]."

### Showcase Invite
*   **The "Save the Date" Logic:** Coaches plan their travel weeks in advance.
*   **Refinement:** Add your **Jersey Number** and **Jersey Color** to the body. If they are on a sideline with 50 kids, they need to know exactly who to look for without checking a roster.

---

## 3. 2024-2026 Best Practices Checklist

| Feature | Best Practice | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Length** | Under 100 words | If they have to scroll on a phone, they might stop reading. |
| **Video** | Link to YouTube/Hudl | Avoid Google Drive links that require "Request Access" (Coaches won't wait). |
| **Profile** | Include a link to a recruiting profile | If you use NCSA, SportsRecruits, or a personal site, put it in your signature. |
| **CCing** | CC your Club/HS Coach | It adds immediate credibility and gives the college coach an easy way to verify you. |
| **Timing** | Tuesday - Thursday mornings | Avoid Monday mornings (inbox clearing) and Friday afternoons (game prep). |

---

## Summary of Changes
1.  **Subject Lines:** Add 1-2 key metrics (Height, Weight, GPA, or a specific stat).
2.  **Personalization:** Make it the "Lead" after the intro.
3.  **Formatting:** Use bullet points for stats to make them pop.
4.  **Action Item:** Be even more specific (e.g., "Do you have any upcoming ID camps where I can play in front of you?").

Your foundation is very strong. These small "pro-level" tweaks are designed to turn an "opened" email into a "replied" email. Good luck with the recruiting process!
